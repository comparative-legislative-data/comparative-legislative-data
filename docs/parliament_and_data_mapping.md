## 1. Core Definition: The Canonical Legislative Measure

To prevent Westminster bias and accommodate non-Westminster systems (e.g. European civil law, presidential executive decree systems, consensus assemblies), we establish a globally neutral primary unit of analysis:

> **Canonical Definition: Primary Legislative Proposal (`LegislativeMeasure`)**  
> *"Any draft statutory text formally introduced into a competent legislative body that, if approved through procedural stages, establishes, amends, or repeals primary statutory law (or constitutional text)."*

### Inclusion vs. Exclusion Standard Matrix

| Measure Category | Included / Excluded | Rationale & Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Bills** | 🟢 **INCLUDED** | Primary government initiatives (e.g. UK Government Bills, German *Gesetzentwürfe*, French *Projets de loi*). |
| **Member / Private Bills** | 🟢 **INCLUDED** | Backbench/opposition initiatives (e.g. UK PMBs, US House/Senate Bills, French *Propositions de loi*). |
| **Executive Decrees with Primary Force** | 🟢 **INCLUDED** | Executive measures enacting primary law subject to legislative ratification (e.g. Brazilian *Medidas Provisórias*, Italian *Decreti-Legge*). |
| **Constitutional Amendment Bills** | 🟢 **INCLUDED** | Draft measures amending constitutional frameworks (e.g. US Joint Resolutions amending Constitution, Indian Constitutional Bills). |
| **Secondary / Subordinate Legislation** | 🔴 **EXCLUDED** | Statutory Instruments (SIs), executive regulations, delegated orders, administrative rules. |
| **Non-Binding Resolutions & Motions** | 🔴 **EXCLUDED** | Commemorative resolutions, procedural adjournment motions, internal parliamentary administration. |

## 2. Peer Review Infrastructure Principles & Provenance Model

Following external academic peer review, Phase 0 enforces three core data generation principles:

1. **Atomic Data Generation over Imposed Indices:** The platform does **not** impose pre-calculated scalar indices (e.g., Executive Dominance Scores). Instead, it generates clean, high-fidelity atomic variables (`author_type`, `author_party_role`, `disposal_outcome`, `rebellions_flag`, `cross_party_sponsorship_count`, `duration_sitting_days`) so researchers can construct custom indices for their specific research designs.
2. **Persistent Member Disambiguation Engine:** Raw sponsor name strings are resolved to stable persistent identifiers (`initiator_member_id`: Wikidata QID / native ID) *before* joining against historical Executive Rosters, eliminating name variation and peerage title errors.
3. **The 3-Tier Data Provenance Model:**
   - **`NATIVE_DIRECT`:** Available directly in the official API/feed payload.
   - **`ENRICHED_BY_PIPELINE`:** Derived & generated by our pipeline (e.g. outcome synthesis, minister joins, sitting days). Includes an explicit `derivation_confidence` level (`HIGH`, `MEDIUM`, `LOW`).
   - **`UNAVAILABLE_GAP`:** Missing natively and cannot be derived (documented Open Data Gap).

## 5. Document Artifact Archiving & Permanent DOI Minting

To guarantee 100% permanent citation URLs for researchers even if host parliament websites redesign or break legacy links:
- **Local Payload Archives:** Raw JSON/XML files are archived in `/data/raw/` with validated 64-character SHA-256 hashes.
- **Institutional Archival Sync (Zenodo / OSF):** Automated periodic sync of raw archives and bulk Parquet releases to Zenodo / Open Science Framework (OSF) to mint **permanent Digital Object Identifiers (DOIs)**.
